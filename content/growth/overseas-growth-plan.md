# Deeper China Overseas Growth Plan

Deeper China should not compete as a generic China travel blog. The growth system should turn specific cultural desire into a concierge conversation: China beyond the obvious, expert-led access, and routes that make hard-to-read places legible.

## Channel Roles

TikTok and Instagram Reels should create demand with visual hooks: Chongqing's 8D city, Chengdu food and pandas, Jingmai tea forests, Wudang Taiji, Jingdezhen studios, Quanzhou Hokkien roots, and Beijing ritual space.

YouTube Shorts should explain one strong idea at a time: why Beijing is not just the Forbidden City, why Quanzhou matters to Southeast Asian Chinese travelers, why Jingdezhen is about process rather than shopping, and why Wudang should not be sold as a kung-fu fantasy.

Google Search should capture decision intent with pages for city comparisons, 3/5/7 day routes, foreigner-specific questions, and expert-led access.

Pinterest should support high-aesthetic discovery for Jingdezhen, Jingmai, Wudang, Quanzhou craft, and slow China itineraries.

Reddit and travel communities should be used carefully for trust-building answers, not direct hard selling. The best angle is to answer difficult itinerary questions with specific tradeoffs.

Paid ads should send traffic to `/chat`, not generic destination pages. The offer should be a route diagnosis or expert-access consultation, not a broad newsletter.

## SEO Structure

The first SEO layer should be decision pages:

- How many days in Beijing for culture travel?
- Chengdu or Chongqing for first-time China visitors?
- Quanzhou vs Xiamen for cultural depth.
- Jingdezhen itinerary for designers and ceramic lovers.
- Wudang Mountain for Taiji beginners.
- Jingmai Mountain tea forest travel guide.

The second layer should be route pages:

- 3 days in Chengdu for pandas and food.
- 5 days in Quanzhou for Maritime Silk Road and Hokkien roots.
- 7 days Beijing and Wudang for ritual and Taoist culture.
- 7 days Jingmai and Jingdezhen for tea and porcelain.

The third layer should be conversion pages:

- Private China itinerary consultation.
- Expert-led China cultural travel.
- Local host introductions in China.
- China travel planning for overseas Chinese roots trips.

## Paid Acquisition

Start with low-budget tests around clear segments:

- Southeast Asian Hokkien diaspora to Quanzhou.
- Families interested in pandas and Chengdu food.
- Visual travelers interested in Chongqing.
- Designers and collectors interested in Jingdezhen.
- Tea and slow-travel audiences interested in Jingmai.
- Taiji and Taoist culture audiences interested in Wudang.

The first conversion should be a short route diagnosis in `/chat`, then lead capture, then paid consultation or expert introduction.

## Content Rules

Every piece of content should answer one hard question or show one unusually specific reason to visit. Avoid generic top-ten phrasing. The voice should be calm, literate, precise, and useful for high-value travelers who want to understand China rather than merely consume attractions.
